The lease was cheap because the previous tenant “left suddenly.” 
That’s what the agent told me while avoiding eye contact, sliding the keys across the table like they burned her fingers.
“Unit 3B,” she said. “You’ll like it. Quiet.”

It was quiet. Too quiet. The apartment sat at the end of a narrow corridor, the kind where sound seems to die halfway down. Inside, everything was already furnished—bed made, dishes stacked, even a half-used bar of soap in the bathroom. It didn’t feel abandoned.
It felt paused. I found a binder on the kitchen counter labeled "tenant notes". Inside were handwritten entries, which were neat at first, then increasingly frantic and disorganized.

Day 1: Pipes make noise at night. I thought it was probably an old building.
Day 3: I heard footsteps in hallway. Maybe there was a neighbor?
Day 5: There are only two units on this floor. I frowned. I’d passed three doors coming in. I flipped further.
Day 8: Someone is using my shower when I’m asleep.
