I laughed nervously, closing the binder. “Cute,” I muttered. Some kind of prank, maybe. Then I checked the bathroom.
The mirror was fogged and there was a wet footprint on the floor.

I didn’t leave that night. Curiosity has a way of overpowering fear when you think you understand it. I told myself there had to be a logical explanation—old pipes, condensation, maybe even a squatter messing with me.
So I stayed up. At 1:12 a.m., I heard it. A soft creak from the hallway. Not outside. Inside.
I stepped out of the bedroom slowly, every nerve alert. The living room was dim, lit only by the streetlamp leaking through the curtains.
That’s when I noticed something wrong. The front door was still locked. But the chain… was swaying. As if it had just been touched. I approached it carefully, then stopped.

There were wet footprints on the floor. Barefoot. Leading from the bathroom… to the hallway.

My chest tightened. I hadn’t showered. I followed them.
