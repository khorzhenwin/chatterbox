They stopped at the wall between the kitchen and the corridor—the one I’d thought separated my unit from the neighboring one.
Except now, up close, I could see it.

A door. Painted over. Sealed shut. My heart pounded as I stepped back.
Behind me, something clicked. I turned.
The bathroom door was open.
Steam curled slowly into the hallway.
And over the sound of dripping water… I heard breathing. Not mine.
I ran to the kitchen, grabbed the binder, flipping desperately through the later entries.
The handwriting was barely legible now.

Day 12: It doesn’t come from outside.
Day 14: There is no Unit 3A.
Day 15: It lives in the space between walls.

My hands trembled.
There had been three doors in the corridor.
But only two units on the floor.
Which meant—

A knock echoed through the apartment. Not from the front door. From inside the wall.
Three slow, deliberate knocks. I backed away, shaking. “No,” I whispered. “No, no—”The wall answered.Another three knocks.
Closer this time.
