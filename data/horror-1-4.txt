The sealed door began to have its paint cracking along invisible seams. 
Something pressed against it from the other side—not breaking through, just… testing.
Then I heard it. My voice.“…you shouldn’t have stayed.”

I stumbled backward, nearly falling. “Who are you?”. 
Then, softer: “I was you.” The binder slipped from my hands. The last page fluttered open on the floor. A final entry.
Not handwritten. Scratched in. 
Day 16: It needs someone to replace it. The wall split. A shape unfolded from the darkness between the walls, too long, too thin, joints bending wrong as it dragged itself into the room. Its face… Was mine.
